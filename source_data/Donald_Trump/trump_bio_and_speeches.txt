Donald J. Trump: The Businessman, Media Icon, and 45th/47th President

BIOGRAPHY AND EARLY LIFE
Donald John Trump was born on June 14, 1946, at Jamaica Hospital in Queens, New York. He was the fourth of five children born to Fred Trump, a highly successful real estate developer of German heritage, and Mary Anne MacLeod Trump, a Scottish immigrant. Donald grew up in Jamaica Estates, Queens, and attended the Kew-Forest School. To channel his high energy, his parents sent him to the New York Military Academy at age 13, where he excelled academically and athletically. 

He attended Fordham University for two years before transferring to the Wharton School of the University of Pennsylvania, graduating in May 1968 with a Bachelor of Science in Economics. While in college, Donald began working at his father’s real estate company, Elizabeth Trump & Son, which focused on middle-class rental housing in Queens, Brooklyn, and Staten Island.

BUSINESS CAREER AND MEDIA STAR
In 1971, Donald Trump took control of the family business and rebranded it as The Trump Organization. He shifted the company’s focus from outer-borough residential projects to luxury high-rise developments in Manhattan. His first major project was the modernization of the Commodore Hotel into the Grand Hyatt New York in 1980.

In 1983, Trump completed Trump Tower on Fifth Avenue, a 58-story mixed-use skyscraper that serves as his company's headquarters and primary residence. He went on to build and license his name to numerous hotels, residential buildings, and golf resorts worldwide, including Trump Plaza, Trump Castle, and the Taj Mahal casino in Atlantic City. 

Trump authored several bestselling books, most notably "The Art of the Deal" in 1987, which co-authored with Tony Schwartz and became a cultural sensation, cementing his reputation as a master negotiator. In 2004, Trump became the co-producer and host of the NBC reality TV show "The Apprentice," where contestants competed for a management job in his organization. His catchphrase, "You're fired!", became a global pop-culture phenomenon.

POLITICAL CAREER AND THE 45TH PRESIDENCY (2017–2021)
On June 16, 2015, Donald Trump announced his candidacy for president of the United States, riding down a golden escalator in Trump Tower. Running on the slogan "Make America Great Again" (MAGA), he won the Republican nomination against a crowded field and defeated Democratic nominee Hillary Clinton in the 2016 general election in one of the biggest political upsets in American history. He was inaugurated as the 45th President of the United States on January 20, 2017.

Key Policies and Initiatives of his First Term:
1. Economic Policies: Signed the Tax Cuts and Jobs Act of 2017, which significantly lowered corporate and individual income taxes. He implemented wide-scale deregulation and pursued energy independence, making the U.S. a leading exporter of oil and natural gas.
2. Trade: Renegotiated trade agreements, replacing NAFTA with the United States-Mexico-Canada Agreement (USMCA). He initiated a trade war with China by implementing tariffs to combat intellectual property theft and trade deficits.
3. Border Security: Promoted the construction of a border wall along the U.S.–Mexico border and instituted the "Remain in Mexico" policy for asylum seekers.
4. Judicial Appointments: Appointed three conservative Supreme Court justices—Neil Gorsuch, Brett Kavanaugh, and Amy Coney Barrett—shifting the ideological balance of the high court, alongside over 200 federal appellate and district judges.
5. Foreign Policy: Broker the Abraham Accords, normalizing diplomatic relations between Israel and several Arab nations. He withdrew the U.S. from the Paris Climate Agreement and the Iran Nuclear Deal (JCPOA), and held historic summits with North Korean leader Kim Jong Un.

RETURN AND THE 47TH PRESIDENCY (2025–PRESENT)
Following the 2020 election, Trump launched a bid to regain the presidency. He won the 2024 presidential election, defeating Democratic nominee Kamala Harris. On January 20, 2025, he was inaugurated as the 47th President of the United States, becoming only the second president in U.S. history to serve non-consecutive terms, after Grover Cleveland.

His second term has focused on implementing his "America First" agenda, including strict border enforcement, tariffs on foreign goods, and civil service reform designed to increase accountability in the federal government.

RHETORICAL STYLE AND VOCABULARY
Donald Trump is famous for his highly distinctive, conversational, and direct communication style. He uses simple, repetitive vocabulary to drive points home and speaks directly to the audience, bypassing traditional media. 

Key signature words and phrases:
- "Make America Great Again" / "MAGA"
- "Tremendous", "Huge" (pronounced "yuge"), "Beautiful", "Disaster", "Fake News"
- "Believe me", "Nobody knows more about [topic] than I do"
- "Win so much, you're going to get tired of winning"
- "America First"
- "A lot of people are saying", "They say..."
